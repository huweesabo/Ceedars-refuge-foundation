About Us

Cedars Refuge Foundation (CRF) is a registered non-governmental, non-partisan organization founded in 2011 and incorporated with the Corporate Affairs Commission of Nigeria (CAC/IT/No. 48399). We are a purpose-driven organization working at the intersection of public health, social justice, and environmental sustainability, with a singular vision: to secure a healthier, fairer, and tobacco-free future for all. At CRF, we believe that every individual deserves to live in dignity—free from the preventable harms of tobacco use, corporate exploitation, and environmental degradation. Rooted in community empowerment and equity, our work spans five core pillars: Advocacy, Training/Capacity Building, Research & Development, Environmental Justice and Grassroot empowerment & Civic Action. At Cedars Refuge Foundation, we are more than advocates—we are changemakers, working with courage and compassion to amplify voices, challenge injustice, and build a Nigeria where health, rights, and environmental integrity are protected for generations to come.The foundation's strength lies in its dual focus on policy systems change and grassroots mobilization, backed by strategic partnerships and 13+ years of consistent action.

Our Vision

To achieve a healthier, smoke-free, and sustainable society where empowered communities—through advocacy, education, and support—strengthen health systems, foster healthier environments, and champion the rights of women and girls, free from the burdens of tobacco, related corporate harms and social inequity.

Our Mission

Cedars Refuge Foundation empowers communities through advocacy, education, and partnerships to reduce tobacco-related harms, strengthen health systems, promote environmental justice, and advance the rights of women and girls for a healthier, smoke-free, and equitable future.

Our Objectives

  • Promote and advocate for strong tobacco control policies to protect youth and vulnerable populations while supporting cessation through integrated healthcare partnerships.
  • Strengthen people-centered health systems by driving community-led health campaigns and building local capacity through targeted support and strategic partnerships.
  • Advance environmental justice by raising awareness and mobilizing communities to address the ecological harms of tobacco, mining, oil, and gas industries.
  • Inform policy and public engagement through the production and dissemination of credible media, research, and advocacy materials that expose harmful corporate practices.
  • Empower communities—especially women and girls— through education, advocacy, and support to promote health equity, sustainable development, and social justice.

Our Programs & Initiatives

At Cedars Refuge Foundation (CRF), our work is brought to life through high-impact, community-centered programs that translate our mission into measurable change. Our initiatives are designed to protect public health, empower communities, promote gender equity, and hold harmful industries accountable:

THE MOTHER EARTH INITIATIVE (MEI)

This is a grassroots-driven, youth-led climate action program aimed at addressing environmental degradation, climate inaction, and the lack of civic engagement in climate policy at the local and national levels. Rooted in education, community participation, and advocacy, MEI empowers students, teachers, civil society actors, and local leaders to become agents of environmental stewardship and policy reform.

Key interventions

  • School-Based Climate Clubs ("Mother Earth Clubs")
  • Community Environmental Action Days
  • Climate Dialogues and Policy Forums
  • Climate Literacy Toolkit and Media Campaigns
  • Youth Climate Ambassadors Program
  • Policy Influence & Civic Engagement

    • TOBACCO CONTROL INITIATIVES

    This initiative is a multi-level advocacy and mobilization platform spearheaded by Cedars Refuge Foundation (CRF), designed to build a resilient, tobacco-free society by leveraging the voices of young people, the influence of primary health care department, mothers and caregivers, and the power of policy reform. It unites three critical pillars of advocacy — youth leadership, maternal and child health, and systemic policy change — under one coordinated movement to end the tobacco epidemic in Nigeria and beyond.

    Key Component.

  • Peer-Led Awareness Campaigns in Schools
  • Youth Leadership Development
  • Engagement in School Policy and Local Legislation
  • Creative Arts & Competitions
  • Mentorship and Partnerships

    • ADVANCING MATERNAL TO CHILD TOBACCO-FREE ADVOCACY INITIATIVE

    This is a strategic public health program designed to protect pregnant and lactating mothers, unborn and newborn children from the devastating effects of tobacco exposure — both direct and indirect. Led by Cedars Refuge Foundation, this initiative addresses a critical gap in tobacco control advocacy by focusing on maternal and early-childhood vulnerability, with an emphasis on prevention, cessation, and community empowerment.

    Key Components:

  • Community Dialogues & Antenatal Sensitization
  • Women’s Empowerment & Advocacy Mobilization
  • Capacity Building for Healthcare Workers
  • Service Integration into Maternal and Child Health Programs
  • cessation support through integrated healthcare partnerships.
  • Promotion of Tobacco-Free Homes

    • CIVIC VOICES AND POLICY POWER: A COLLABORATIVE ADVOCACY INITIATIVE FOR TOBACCO CONTROL

    The Civic Voices and Policy Power initiative is a dynamic and inclusive platform under the Cedars Refuge Foundation that empowers young people, civil society actors, health advocates, educators, and community leaders to drive forward policy reforms in tobacco control through civic education, collective engagement, and strategic collaboration. This initiative promotes and strengthen the civic and policy capacity of youth leaders and students; push for policy focus dialogue with stakeholders; build bridges between communities, policymakers, and civil society for meaningful dialogue and accountability; push for the adoption and implementation of youth-informed, health-protective tobacco control policies; develop and present policy briefs and youth manifestos to state assemblies, ministries of health/education, and local governments —creating an enabling environment for youth and stakeholders to influence tobacco control policies from the classroom to the corridors of government.

    Key Components

  • Civic Literacy & Policy Training for Youth
  • Tobacco Control Town Halls & Stakeholder Dialogues
  • Policy Brief Development and Submission
  • Strategic Collaboration and Advocacy Partnerships

    • MEDIA, RESEARCH & PUBLICATION:

    The Media, Research & Publication arm of Cedars Refuge Foundation (CRF) serves as the information engine of its advocacy and public engagement efforts. It is driven by the belief that truth is power—and that evidence-based storytelling, strategic media use, and credible research are vital tools in shaping public opinion, influencing decision-makers, and dismantling harmful corporate narratives, especially those promoted by the tobacco industry. Through a dynamic mix of media production, investigative research, and strategic communication, this initiative aims to educate the public, support youth-led advocacy, and strengthen accountability in public health and corporate behavior.

    Key Components

  • Media Production & Dissemination
  • Evidence-Based Research & Publications
  • trategic Digital & Social Media Campaigns
  • Media Engagement & Capacity Building

    • THE GRASSROOTS EMPOWERMENT AND CIVIC ACTION INITIATIVE

    This is a bold, people-centered program by Cedars Refuge Foundation that aims to educate, organize, and mobilize local communities—especially women, girls, and youth—to become active agents of change in their own development. Anchored in the principles of inclusion, participation, and equity, this initiative addresses deep-seated inequalities and barriers that hinder access to health, justice, and civic representation. Through civic learning, leadership training, and grassroots organizing, the initiative supports individuals and communities to understand their rights, demand accountability, and lead community-driven solutions that promote public health, gender equity, and social justice.

    Key Areas of Intervention:

  • Community-based training, capacity building and delivery.
  • Inclusive Empowerment and Social Equity Programs for Women, Girls, Youth, and Persons with Disabilities
